中国 China

Discover China's Wonders

Embark on a journey through China's most iconic landmarks, where ancient history meets natural beauty. Each destination tells a unique story of China's rich cultural heritage and architectural mastery.

UNESCO Heritage Mountain Views Hiking
Beijing, China

The Great Wall

Best Time: Mar-May, Sep-Oct Walking Time: 2-4 hours Peak Season: Spring/Fall

One of the Seven Wonders of the World, the Great Wall stretches over 13,000 miles across China's northern borders. Built over 2,000 years ago, this magnificent structure served as both a military defense and border control, showcasing ancient Chinese engineering brilliance.

Highlights

  • Stunning mountain vistas and panoramic views
  • Perfect spots for sunrise and sunset photography
  • Well-preserved ancient watchtowers and fortifications
  • Beautiful seasonal changes in surrounding landscapes
Imperial Palace Heritage Site Museum
Central Beijing

The Forbidden City

Visit Duration: 3-4 hours Built: 1406-1420 980 Buildings

The world's largest palace complex, spanning 72 hectares with 980 buildings. This UNESCO World Heritage site served as the imperial palace for 24 emperors during the Ming and Qing dynasties, representing the zenith of Chinese architectural achievement and imperial power.

Highlights

  • Magnificent imperial throne halls and royal quarters
  • Extensive collection of Chinese imperial treasures
  • Exquisite ancient artworks and calligraphy
  • Traditional Chinese palace architecture
Natural Wonder Scenic Views Hot Springs
Huangshan, Anhui

Yellow Mountain

Best Season: Spring/Autumn Height: 1,864m Recommended Stay: 2-3 days

Renowned for its "Four Wonders": peculiar pine trees, spectacular granite peaks, hot springs, and the mystical sea of clouds. Yellow Mountain has inspired Chinese art and literature for millennia with its breathtaking landscapes and ethereal beauty.

Highlights

  • Enchanting sea of clouds and misty peaks
  • Ancient pine trees in dramatic poses
  • Relaxing natural hot springs
  • Spectacular sunrise and sunset views
Ancient Army Archaeological Site Cultural Heritage
Xi'an, Shaanxi

The Terracotta Army

Visit Duration: 4-5 hours Built: 210-209 BC 8,000+ Warriors

An extraordinary archaeological find, the Terracotta Army features thousands of life-sized clay warriors guarding Emperor Qin's tomb. Each soldier has unique facial features, representing one of the most remarkable achievements in ancient Chinese art and craftsmanship.

Highlights

  • Detailed life-sized terracotta warriors
  • Ancient military equipment and horses
  • On-site restoration workshop
  • Interactive museum exhibits
