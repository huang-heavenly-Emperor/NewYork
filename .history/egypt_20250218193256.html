مصر Egypt
"Walk like an Egyptian through the sands of time"
Egypt, located in North Africa, is renowned for its ancient civilization and iconic landmarks like the Pyramids of Giza, the Sphinx, and the Nile River. As one of the world's oldest cultures, it has a rich history of pharaohs, hieroglyphics, and monumental architecture. Modern Egypt blends its historic heritage with vibrant cities like Cairo, known for its bustling markets and Islamic architecture. The country is also a gateway to the Sahara Desert and the Red Sea, offering diverse landscapes and cultural experiences. Egyptian cuisine, featuring dishes like koshari and falafel, reflects its unique cultural blend.

Giza, Egypt

The Great Pyramids

2560 BC 146.5m

The Great Pyramids of Giza are the only surviving wonder of the ancient world, built as eternal resting places for the pharaohs. The largest pyramid, built for Pharaoh Khufu, stood at 146.5 meters high and remained the tallest man-made structure for over 3,800 years.

Highlights

  • World's oldest wonder of ancient world
  • Built with over 2.3 million stone blocks
  • Perfect astronomical alignment
Giza Plateau

The Great Sphinx

2500 BC 20m

The Great Sphinx of Giza, with the head of a human and body of a lion, stands as an iconic symbol of ancient Egyptian civilization. This limestone statue, believed to represent Pharaoh Khafre, has guarded the pyramids for over 4,500 years.

Highlights

  • Largest monolithic statue
  • Mysterious ancient guardian
  • Symbol of royal power
Egypt

The Nile River

6,650 km Fertile Valley

The Nile River, the world's longest river, has been Egypt's lifeline since ancient times. Its annual floods brought fertile soil, creating the rich Nile Valley where ancient Egyptian civilization flourished. Today, it continues to sustain modern Egypt's agriculture and way of life.

Highlights

  • World's longest river
  • Cradle of civilization
  • Luxury river cruises
