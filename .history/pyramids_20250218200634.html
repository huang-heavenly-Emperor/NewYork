الأهرامات The Great Pyramids

The Great Pyramids of Giza, built over 4,500 years ago, stand as eternal monuments to ancient Egyptian civilization, engineering mastery, and the pharaohs' quest for immortality.

Historical Overview

The Great Pyramids of Giza were built during the Old Kingdom period of Ancient Egypt, around 2560 BCE. The largest and most famous pyramid was built for Pharaoh Khufu and took approximately 20 years to complete. Standing at an original height of 146.5 meters (481 feet), it remained the tallest man-made structure for over 3,800 years.

The pyramids served as elaborate tombs for the pharaohs, designed to protect their bodies and possessions for the afterlife. The complex engineering and astronomical precision of their construction continues to amaze scholars and visitors alike.

Construction Marvel

Precise Planning

The pyramids were built with incredible precision, aligned to true north with an accuracy of 0.15 degrees.

Advanced Engineering

Massive limestone blocks were cut with copper chisels and transported using wooden sleds and rollers.

Skilled Workforce

An estimated 20,000-30,000 workers were involved in the construction, working in shifts throughout the year.

Astronomical Alignment

The pyramids' sides are perfectly aligned with the four cardinal points of the compass.
